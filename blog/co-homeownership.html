What is Co-Homeownership with reSpace? Redefining Homeownership for Modern Living

You already pay enough to own. Now you can.

November 2024 Katrina Romatowski 7 min read

What Is Structured Co-Homeownership?

The housing market told an entire generation to wait. Wait for prices to drop. Wait for rates to come down. Wait for the right time. reSpace stopped waiting.

Structured co-homeownership is a new category of homeownership where buyers own a private suite in a shared luxury property. Each co-owner holds a membership interest in a single-purpose LLC, with exclusive-use rights to their private suite and shared-use rights to common areas — the living room, outdoor spaces, and a fully equipped shared area.

It's not co-living. It's not a timeshare. It's not a compromise. It's ownership — structured, legal, and designed for the way people actually want to live.

How Does the reSpace Model Work?

reSpace takes single-family homes in premier Seattle neighborhoods and transforms them through technology and design into small communities of individual owners.

Each co-owner gets a private suite with an ensuite bathroom, walk-in closet, private washer/dryer, wet bar and coffee station, sitting area, and workspace. You own your space. Period.

The shared spaces — designed by architects who understand how community actually functions — include gathering areas, outdoor living spaces, and amenities that would cost a fortune in a traditional home.

Monthly costs are all-inclusive. No surprise assessments. No hidden fees. Just one number that covers everything — and it's comparable to what you'd pay renting a one-bedroom apartment in the same neighborhood.

"reSpace provides homeownership in the best homes and neighborhoods for what people are already paying to rent a one-bedroom." — Katrina Romatowski, Founder & CEO

Why Is This Different from Everything Else?

Co-homeownership isn't renting with a fancy name. It's a legal ownership structure with real wealth-building potential. Your membership interest is yours. You retain full appreciation on your share.

It's not a commune. Each suite is private, designed for independent living with the community spaces as a bonus — not a requirement.

And it's not just for one type of buyer. Remote professionals recalibrating where they live. Long-distance commuters who need a Seattle base. Young professionals tired of watching their rent vanish. Retirees who want community without giving up independence.

By the numbers: 70% of Gen Z and 48% of all Americans are open to co-buying. The demand isn't a question. The supply was the missing piece. reSpace is the supply.

What Does Ownership Actually Look Like Day-to-Day?

You come home to your private suite. Your space. Your door. Your quiet. When you want community, it's steps away — shared dinners, morning coffee on the porch, a neighbor who actually knows your name.

When you want solitude, you close your door. It's that simple.

reSpace designs the balance between privacy and community into the architecture itself. This isn't accidental togetherness. It's intentional design that gives people what they actually need: both.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is co-homeownership?

Co-homeownership is a structured ownership model where multiple buyers each own a membership interest in a single-purpose LLC, with exclusive-use rights to a private suite and shared-use rights to common areas in a property.

How is co-homeownership different from renting?

Co-owners hold a real ownership stake with full appreciation retained on their share. Unlike renting, your monthly payment builds toward something you own — not someone else's wealth.

Is co-homeownership the same as co-living?

No. Co-living implies rental and transient arrangements. Co-homeownership is a legal ownership structure where each person owns their private suite and shares common areas. You own — you don't rent.

How much does it cost to co-own with reSpace?

Ownership starts at price points comparable to renting a one-bedroom apartment in the same neighborhood. reSpace's first collection, The Leschi Collection, offers suites starting at accessible price points with all-inclusive monthly costs.

Where does reSpace operate?

reSpace currently operates in Seattle's premier neighborhoods, with The Leschi Collection as its first property. Expansion plans include additional Seattle neighborhoods like Ballard, Capitol Hill, and Fremont.
