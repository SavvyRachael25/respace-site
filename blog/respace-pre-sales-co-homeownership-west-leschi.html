reSpace Launches Pre-Sales for Co-Homeownership in West Leschi

Emily Marek · Mar 3, 2026 · 4 min read
Article Image

reSpace, a Seattle-based company redefining what homeownership looks like in high-cost markets, has launched pre-sales for its first property — a Victorian mansion in West Leschi that will become a structured co-homeownership community of five private suites.

The project, called The Leschi Collection, offers ownership starting at $124,500 — a fraction of what a traditional single-family home in the neighborhood commands. Traditional homes in West Leschi start at $1.5 million or more. reSpace is not targeting the same buyer. It is creating a new one.

The Property

The Victorian Mansion on the Hill sits in West Leschi, one of Seattle's most sought-after residential neighborhoods. The property has been thoughtfully converted to support five co-homeowners, each with a private suite and access to shared living spaces designed for the way people actually live.

Shared spaces include a designer kitchen, a co-working space, a lower level media lounge, and a landscaped backyard. Private suites range from entry-level ownership at $124,500 to flagship suites at $249,000, depending on the floor, views, and configuration.

Why It Matters

Seattle's housing affordability crisis is not a new story. Median home prices in the city have climbed for more than a decade, pricing out a growing share of the workforce — including many of the professionals who make the city function. Teachers, nurses, small business owners, and creative workers have increasingly found themselves in a market that does not have a product designed for them.

reSpace is building that product. Co-homeownership is not a compromise. It is a restructured model of ownership that delivers a private residence, shared amenities, and real equity — for what many of these buyers are already paying in rent.

You already pay enough to own. The structure just has to match the reality of how people live and what they can afford to buy.

How It Works

Each co-homeowner receives a membership interest in a single-purpose LLC that holds the property. Ownership is individual — co-owners have their own loans, their own titles, and are not financially tied to one another. This is not a shared mortgage. It is not a tenancy-in-common arrangement that requires unanimous consent to sell. Each owner can exit independently.

Monthly costs include the home loan, property taxes, insurance, maintenance, landscaping, housekeeping, and professional property management. The model is designed so that co-homeowners experience the benefits of ownership — appreciation, equity, stability — without the operational burden of being a landlord or managing a complex legal structure on their own.

In-house financing is available, with approval timelines designed for buyers who do not fit the traditional W-2 profile. Self-employed buyers, gig workers, and entrepreneurs are explicitly part of the target market.

What's Next

Open houses are scheduled for two weekends in March — March 21 through 22 and March 28 through 29 — with weekday viewings by appointment. reSpace has indicated that additional properties are in the pipeline, with the West Leschi project serving as the model for a broader expansion into other Seattle neighborhoods and eventually other cities.

The company is also actively working with real estate brokers, framing co-homeownership as a legitimate and compelling option for buyers who have been priced out of the traditional market. Broker co-op structures and dedicated support are available for agents bringing clients to the property.

The Leschi Collection is open.
