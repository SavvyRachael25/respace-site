Breaking Barriers, Building Futures: Carmen Best Takes the Stage at Shoulders of Giants

Event Image

On April 16, 2026, Carmen Best — former Chief of Police for the Seattle Police Department and one of the most recognized leaders in the country — will take the stage at Columbia Tower Club for a Shoulders of Giants conversation. The event is titled Breaking Barriers, Building Futures. It is a reSpace and KERE production.

This is not a panel. This is not a conference. This is a conversation about leadership, legacy, and what it looks like to build something that lasts — featuring one of the people who has actually done it.

What is Shoulders of Giants?

Shoulders of Giants is a conversation series built around the belief that the best way to honor the people who paved the way is to tell their stories — out loud, in rooms full of people who need to hear them. Each event features a leader whose life and work have shaped the community around them. The format is intimate. The conversations are real.

Breaking Barriers, Building Futures is the latest in the series. Carmen Best is the right person for this moment.

Why Carmen Best

Carmen Best served as Chief of the Seattle Police Department from 2018 to 2020. She was the first Black woman to hold that position in Seattle's history. Her tenure was defined by one of the most consequential periods in modern American policing — a time that tested institutions, leadership, and the people inside them.

She did not step back from the difficulty. She led through it. And when she left, she did not disappear. She kept showing up — as an author, as a speaker, as someone who continues to shape the conversation about what leadership actually requires.

A Shoulders of Giants conversation celebrating leadership, community, and the people shaping the future.

The Cause: Rise Up Academy

Proceeds from Breaking Barriers, Building Futures support Rise Up Academy — an organization committed to creating pathways for young people who have been underserved by traditional systems. The work is direct and the impact is measurable. When you reserve a seat, you are not just attending an event. You are funding a program that changes outcomes for real kids in this city.

That alignment is intentional. reSpace was built on the belief that access — to ownership, to opportunity, to the rooms where decisions get made — should not be determined by who you already know or how much you already have. Rise Up Academy is doing that same work in a different arena.

FAQ

Is this event open to the public?
Yes. Tickets are available through Givebutter. Seating is limited — reserve early.
Where does my ticket purchase go?
All proceeds support Rise Up Academy, an organization creating educational and economic pathways for underserved youth in the Seattle area.
What is Columbia Tower Club?
Columbia Tower Club is Seattle's premier private club, located on floors 75 and 76 of the Columbia Center — the tallest building in Seattle. The 76th floor offers panoramic views of the city, Puget Sound, and the Olympic and Cascade mountain ranges.
What is the format of the event?
A seated lunch followed by a moderated Shoulders of Giants conversation with Carmen Best, followed by Q&A. The format is designed to be intimate and conversational — not a keynote, not a panel. A real exchange.
Who is producing this event?
Breaking Barriers, Building Futures is a reSpace and KERE production, presented in partnership with Columbia Tower Club.
