A Look Into the Future of Ownership with the reSpace Reveal

We didn't just tell people what co-homeownership looks like. We showed them, in augmented reality.

June 2, 2025 Katrina Romatowski 5 min read

What Was the reSpace Reveal?

On a Friday afternoon in Ballard, something unprecedented happened. Guests stood on the future site of The Grove, an empty lot with nothing but dirt and potential, and walked through their future homes. In augmented reality.

The reSpace Reveal was the first-ever immersive experience where potential co-owners could explore The Grove before a single wall was built. Powered by tkei, a spatial computing platform created by The Little Things AI, the experience transported guests into a fully rendered version of what their community would become.

How Did the AR/VR Experience Work?

Guests were outfitted with AR devices and walked the physical site while seeing the finished property overlaid in real time. Private suites. Common areas. Outdoor spaces. The architecture. The materials. The light.

It wasn't a slideshow. It wasn't a render on a screen. It was spatial, you moved through the space, turned corners, looked out windows that didn't exist yet. The future of ownership, made tangible before the foundation was poured.

"We didn't want to describe The Grove. We wanted people to stand inside it."

Why Does This Matter for Co-Homeownership?

Real estate has always been sold on imagination, floor plans, renderings, promises. reSpace Reveal removed the imagination gap entirely. When you can walk through your future home, ownership stops being abstract. It becomes real.

And when ownership feels real, people act. The reSpace Reveal wasn't just an event. It was the moment The Grove went from concept to community.

Frequently Asked Questions

What was the reSpace Reveal?

The reSpace Reveal was an immersive AR/VR event held at the future site of The Grove in Ballard, Seattle, where guests walked through the co-homeownership community in augmented reality before construction.

What technology powered the reSpace Reveal?

The experience was powered by tkei, a spatial computing platform created by The Little Things AI, enabling real-time augmented reality walkthroughs of the future property.

What is The Grove?

The Grove is reSpace's co-homeownership community in Crown Hill (Ballard), Seattle, featuring eight private residences with ownership suites ranging from $179,000 to $299,950.
