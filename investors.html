The model

How reSpace works.

reSpace uses technology and design to transform single-family homes in premier neighborhoods into small communities of individual owners. Each co-owner holds a membership interest in a single-purpose LLC with exclusive-use rights to a private suite and shared-use rights to common areas.

The model is designed for owner-occupancy, not short-term rentals or investor speculation. In-house financing serves non-traditional borrowers with approvals in 7 to 10 days.

Key differentiators

  • Primary-residence focused, not vacation or second-home
  • In-house financing for non-traditional borrowers
  • Single-purpose LLC structure per property
  • Owner-occupied only, no short-term rentals
  • 3% buyer broker commission through escrow
Ownership structure

Governance and capital reserves.

Joint Venture functions as a contractual allocation of rights and obligations. Each co-homeowner purchases a membership interest in the single-purpose LLC that owns the property. This represents shared ownership of the entire home, not a separate unit.

Co-homeowners receive exclusive-use rights to a designated suite and shared-use rights to common areas. They participate in governance and decision-making. Ownership interests may be transferred subject to operating agreement procedures, with right of first refusal for existing co-owners.

Capital reserves

  • Dedicated operating reserves for maintenance and repairs
  • Insurance and property tax funded in advance
  • $7,500 membership fee per incoming co-homeowner for common area furnishing and reserve building
  • Closing through escrow at Mauseth Legal

The reSpace Foundation

Community capital.

The reSpace Foundation is a 501(c)(3) that receives 1% from every property sale. This capital funds housing-focused grants and community development in the neighborhoods where reSpace operates.

The Foundation demonstrates mission alignment: reSpace is not just a business. It is a wealth-building movement.
